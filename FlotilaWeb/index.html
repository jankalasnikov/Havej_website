How did you become a friend with code

The initial spark that has developed into my ongoing passion for IT were, despite seeming as a banality, PC games that I used to play almost every day as a child. I started gaming plainly for fun, but I was soon inquiring how to improve the experience, so I started to research and get interested in hardware. As I wasn’t able to afford a high-end PC at the time, I took a part job over the summer holidays and made my first money that allowed me to start to build a truly professional PC that I desired. This positive experience had further motivated me to learn more, so I applied for a specialised IT seminar that my school was offering to a few students. One of the topics that we got engaged in was Web development. I came to see how much I can use and express my own creativity and ideas in this field and I began to be confident that I want to see my future in IT industry.

What about your aspirations for the future?

The world around us is constantly evolving, and no doubt modern technology is in charge. I am constantly amazed what can be developed through code I do not want to be just a direct observer of how the world is changing under my hands, or how my home country is improving in the European DESI index based on modern technology. I want to be a direct participant in these changes and I want to contribute with my knowledge and skills to these inevitable changes. I want to act not just observe. I want that people around the world would have the same possibilities as people here in Denmark as I was able to observe how developed society functions. My goal is to be a developer and learn how modern companies/start-ups function nowadays, and how they treat their programmers. Later on I could be in charge as SCRUM master or Project manager and lead the development by myself and be true a professional in this area. My next step after graduating at UCN will be Masters in Information Systems Management at University of Economics in Prague. If things go well I would like to take MBA title as well as it would teach me how to properly manage people and organizations.
